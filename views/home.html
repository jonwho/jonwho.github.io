Computer Science?

At first it was because I was undecided. I was a junior in high school yet I had no idea what I wanted to do for a living. I bounced around the idea of being a pharamcist or dentist. But those careers never really appealed to me like CS did. I was simply on the computer most of the time and everyone has that family member that they would ask for computer help, and that person was me! I mean I was good at it because I always tinkered with my computers.

I never really thought about CS being a career until I watched a lecture given by Randy Pausch called "The Last Lecture". And what really hit me wasn't that he was a CS professor, but what he left behind was something significant. He changed the lives of his students and colleagues alike. And I wanted that kind of success. I wanted the success that comes with being great at your field of work. As soon as I wrote my first C program in high school, it really sunk in that this is my career, a career that impact the daily interactions of people I'll never meet. It is meaningful and enduring.

Hackathon

I placed 2nd place in a challenge called Code Golf for programmers using C. The challenge was to implement the Leibniz Sum with the shortest amount of characters (whitespace & special characters included!).

This challenge made me look back at all the knowledge of C I had, the little quirks about it when it comes to expressions, functions and headers. Ultimately I put my code into 1 line and used a ternary op to cut out if-else condition and that shortened the code by a lot. I made sure to cut out all extra variables and reuse only 2 variables to loop and keep track of the Leibniz Sum.

In the end I was only behind the next C developer by .2 points. Which is something along the lines of 2 characters! So close yet so far.
